# Credit Cards & Credit History — Key Takeaways

## 1. Credit score does NOT transfer country to country
- Indian credit history generally does not carry over to countries like Japan.
- In Japan, you will most likely start with no local credit history.

## 2. What will likely happen if moving to Japan
- First few months: limited/no credit history.
- Possible initial credit card rejection (normal for newcomers).
- Stable job + salary + local bank account matter much more.
- Within ~6–18 months, things usually normalize.

## 3. Dad’s credit score cannot be transferred
- Credit score belongs to the person, not the card.
- You cannot inherit or transfer his credit history.
- You may use his card (if allowed), but the score impact stays on his profile.

## 4. Credit cards are worth understanding — not obsessing over
Learn once:
- Pay full bill on time.
- Keep utilization reasonable.
- Avoid unnecessary debt.
- Build good habits.

Then automate it.

## 5. Is card knowledge useful?
Yes, but only to a point.

A financially aware person with good cards may gain:
- Cashback/rewards
- Travel perks
- Better convenience
- Easier access to credit

But:
- Career growth
- Salary increases
- Investing
- Avoiding bad debt

usually matter far more financially.

## 6. Practical rule
Be in the **top 20% of users**, not the top 1%.

Good setup:
- 1–2 solid cards
- Full payment every month
- No interest payments
- No reward-chasing spending

Goal: **Use credit cards as a tool, not a hobby.**
