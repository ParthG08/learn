# 🇯🇵 Japanese NISA: Essential Guide (2024 Reformed System)

## 1. Core Overview
The **Nippon Individual Savings Account (NISA)** is a tax-advantaged investment program for residents of Japan. As of 2024, the system is permanent, meaning there is no expiry date on the tax-exempt status of your holdings.

## 2. The Two-Quota Structure
You can invest in both quotas simultaneously under one account:


| Feature | **Tsumitate (Accumulation) Quota** | **Growth Quota** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Annual Limit** | ¥1.2 Million | ¥2.4 Million |
| **Total Annual Max** | \--- Combined: ¥3.6 Million --- | |
| **Investment Type** | Low-cost, Diversified Mutual Funds | Stocks, ETFs, REITs, Mutual Funds |
| **Purchase Method** | Automated Monthly "Set-and-Forget" | Lump Sum or Systematic |

## 3. Lifetime Investment Limits
*   **Total Limit:** **¥18 Million** per person.
*   **Growth Sub-limit:** Within that ¥18M, only **¥12 Million** can be used for the Growth Quota.
*   **Limit "Recycling":** If you sell an asset, the **book value** (original purchase price) of that asset is added back to your lifetime limit the **following year**.

## 4. Eligibility & Residency
*   **Age:** 18 years or older.
*   **Requirement:** Must be a **Tax Resident** of Japan (holds a valid Residence Card and My Number).
*   **PR/Citizenship:** **NOT required**. Holders of work, spouse, or long-term visas are eligible.
*   **Leaving Japan:** If you permanently leave Japan, you generally must close the account or convert it to a taxable account, losing the tax-free benefits.

## 5. Tax Benefits & Restrictions
*   **Tax Savings:** 0% tax on capital gains and dividends (instead of the standard **20.315%**).
*   **No Loss-Harvesting:** Losses inside a NISA cannot be used to offset gains in a regular taxable brokerage account.
*   **U.S. Citizens:** Use extreme caution. Most NISA funds are **PFICs** (Passive Foreign Investment Companies) and may result in punitive U.S. tax reporting and liabilities.

## 6. How to Get Started
1.  **Select ONE Broker:** You can only have one NISA account at a time (e.g., SBI Securities, Rakuten Securities, or Interactive Brokers for English support).
2.  **Documents:** Prepare your **My Number Card** (or Notification Card + Juminhyo) and your **Residence Card**.
3.  **Application:** The approval process usually takes 1–3 weeks as the tax office must verify you don't have a NISA elsewhere.



## Comparison: Singapore SRS vs. Japan New NISA

| Feature | Singapore SRS | Japan New NISA |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Annual Limit** | **S$15,300** (SC/PR) <br> **S$35,700** (Foreigners) | **¥3.6 million** <br> (Approx. S$31,000) |
| **Lifetime Limit** | **None** <br> (Limited only by annual cap) | **¥18 million** <br> (Approx. S$155,000) |
| **Primary Tax Benefit** | **Immediate Tax Relief**: Contributions reduce your taxable income today. | **Tax-Free Gains**: No tax on dividends or capital gains forever. |
| **Withdrawal Rule** | **Restricted**: Taxed at 50% after age 63; 5% penalty + 100% tax if withdrawn early. | **Flexible**: Withdraw anytime with no tax or penalties. |
| **Reusability** | N/A (One-way contribution) | **Reusable**: Selling assets frees up your lifetime limit space for the next year. |
