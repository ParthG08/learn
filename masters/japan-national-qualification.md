# Japanese National IT Qualifications (国家資格) - Complete Guide

This document summarizes the Information-Technology Engineers Examination (ITEE) system, its benefits for software engineers, and strategies for Permanent Residency (PR) points.

## 1. Hierarchy of Qualifications (IPA)
Administered by the Information-technology Promotion Agency (IPA), the exams are categorized into four levels:

| Level | Exam Name | Japanese Name | Target Audience |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **L1** | IT Passport (IP) | ITパスポート | Entry-level literacy |
| **L2** | Fundamental IT (FE) | 基本情報技術者 | Junior Engineers / Programmers |
| **L3** | Applied IT (AP) | 応用情報技術者 | Mid-level / Senior Engineers |
| **L4** | Advanced (Specialist) | 高度試験 | Architects, DB, Network Specialists |

## 2. Permanent Residency (PR) & Visa Benefits
The "Highly Skilled Professional" (HSP) point system provides significant shortcuts to PR based on these exams.

* **Point Allocation:**
    * **1 Exam cleared:** 5 Points.
    * **2+ Exams cleared:** 10 Points (Maximum).
* **PR Fast-Track:**
    * **70 Points:** Eligible for PR after 3 years.
    * **80 Points:** Eligible for PR after 1 year.
* **Note:** Higher-level exams (L3/L4) do not grant *more* points than L2, but they allow you to reach the 10-point cap if you hold two different certifications.

## 3. Career Alignment: Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE)
For those aiming for FDE roles, the following exams are most relevant:
* **Applied IT Engineer (AP):** Focuses on "System Strategy" and "Project Management"—critical for deploying solutions at client sites.
* **Network Specialist (NW):** Essential for system integrations, API management, and webhooks.
* **Database Specialist (DB):** Critical for data transformations and customer insights.

## 4. Practical Information for Students
* **Eligibility:** No restrictions on age, nationality, or education. You can take these while enrolled in a Master's program.
* **Language:** L1 & L2 are available via CBT (Computer-Based Testing). L3 & L4 are paper-based (April/October) and primarily in Japanese.
* **ITPEC (Outside Japan):** If outside Japan, exams can be taken in English in specific countries (Vietnam, Thailand, etc.) and are legally recognized in Japan.

## 5. Resources
* **Official Website:** [IPA.go.jp](https://www.ipa.go.jp/shiken/index.html)
* **Study Materials:** Free past question papers (*Kako-mon*) and answer keys are available on the IPA website for all levels.
